Ornaments, on a tree, hang permanently.

Whether unique, plan, or weird, all will succumb,

Once hung, a part of the tree it does become.

Out on the lawn, a tree, a new memory,

Ornaments still bound to each extremity.

No one paid any mind to leaving them behind,

Cheap decorations, held no expectations.

And next year’s would be better, don’t ya know.

Except in one house, with a petulant child,

With an funny attachment, most would find wild.

The start of each new year would be full of tears.

Pleads for one more day, before they go away.

But no was ever said, as the tree stood dead,

And a small heart would break all over again.

No one could tell from where this obsession came,

But most came to know the child, #name#, by fame.

Over the years #name# grew out of tears.

Growing and able, and proud of the label.

A few, #name# would steal, and some pain did heal,

A collection, in secret, started to grow.

It soon was found out, and after a long shout,

Concessions were made, and the weirdness did fade,

After all, people did collect stranger things.

First it was funny and cost little money,

And no greater thanks would a kid ever give.

When searching for the best gift, it was well known,

Reputation spread, how the passion had grown.

The years quickly past, as the collection grew,

And a new friend, found love, made one out of two.

Foundations were soon laid, and new lives now made.

The ornaments would stay safely packed away,

Save for once a year, and for only a day,

Out they would stay, on a table for display.

And told glory of each origin story.

With a happy small tear and waiting each year.

But the absence of a tree simply wouldn’t do.

We’ll cut off the branches and put them away,

Next year, adhere, the tree with last year’s array.

Each year it’d get bigger, and yet they’d figure,

Ropes, stands, and ladders while keeping what matters.

Every year, with a tear, #name# would observe,

Each ornament hanging safely preserved.

Old ones and new ones, with little space for show.

Branches strung to branches causing them to curve.

Following each snow, it continued to grow.

It had now come to be quite a monstrous tree,

A cringe worthy sample of topiary.

What they would do the next year they did not know.

More structure, more rigging, and starting in June,

They’ll need a shipping container, someday soon.

Regarding them now, with some ninety years past,

The relations, and loved ones that did not last.

All the ornaments, not one of them was lost,

Needed, soon, to be hung, no matter the cost.

Some neighbors came to aid the lonely elder,

With a large effort, they managed the welter.

Out on the lawn, for the whole world to come see,

A structure inspiring curiosity.

A single child remained to ascertain,

The secret reason for this Christmas treason.

One after one, #name# would stay, and relay,

A story of love, not lightly cast away.

Memories are like ornaments, you see,

On a heart, they hang, permanently.
